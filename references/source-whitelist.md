# Source Whitelist

Sources the Researcher (Stage 1) is permitted to use. Anything outside this list is forbidden unless the client explicitly extends the whitelist.

## Tier 1 — International top-tier publications

- Reuters
- Associated Press (AP)
- Bloomberg
- Financial Times (FT)
- The Wall Street Journal (WSJ)
- The New York Times (NYT)
- The Economist
- BBC News
- The Guardian

## Tier 2 — Authoritative trade and business publications

- Forbes
- Fortune
- TechCrunch
- Wired
- The Atlantic
- Sector-specific journals with editorial control:
  - Nature, Science (research)
  - Harvard Business Review (management)
  - The Lancet, NEJM (medicine)
  - Variety, The Hollywood Reporter (film)
  - Billboard (music industry)
  - Architectural Digest (architecture)
  - and similar editorial publications in other professional domains
- Local Tier 1 publications of the person's country:
  - Corriere della Sera, La Repubblica (Italy)
  - Le Monde, Les Echos (France)
  - Der Spiegel, Frankfurter Allgemeine (Germany)
  - Asahi Shimbun, Nikkei (Japan)
  - South China Morning Post (Hong Kong / China)
  - Folha de S.Paulo (Brazil)
  - El País (Spain)
  - and analogous national-tier publications in other countries

## Authoritative profiles and registries

- **Wikipedia** — only as a starting point; every fact pulled from Wikipedia requires cross-validation against another whitelisted source (Tier 1, Tier 2, or authoritative registry).
- **LinkedIn** — official profile of the person or their organization
- **Crunchbase** — for business facts about companies and founders
- **Corporate "About" / "Leadership" pages** — on official organization sites
- **Academic bio pages** — on universities and research institution sites
- **Government registries**:
  - SEC filings (US public companies)
  - Companies House (UK)
  - National patent office registries
  - Official government websites
- **Official international organization pages**:
  - UN, WHO, IMF, World Bank, OECD
  - WIPO, WTO, IAEA
  - Other recognized multilateral institutions

## Forbidden sources

Never use these as primary or supporting sources:

- Personal blogs and Substack
- Medium articles
- Forums: Reddit, Quora, Stack Exchange
- Press releases not picked up by Tier 1/2 publications
- Data aggregators without cross-validation: Owler, ZoomInfo, RocketReach, PitchBook
- Tabloids: Daily Mail, The Sun, NY Post, TMZ, Page Six
- AI-generated wiki clones and biography compilation sites
- IMDb — only as supplementary verification, never as sole source
- The person's own social media (Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook posts) as a source of facts about them
  - Exception: verifying direct quotes already attributed to that social account in a Tier 1/2 source

## Photo source whitelist

Photos may only come from:
- Wikipedia / Wikimedia Commons (free licenses)
- Official corporate "About" pages (editorial use understood)
- Official academic bio pages on university sites

Forbidden photo sources:
- Reuters, Getty Images, AP Photo, and other stock agencies
- Social media (any platform)
- Random websites or aggregator sites

## Confidence levels

When citing a fact, assign confidence based on the source set:
- `high` — 2+ Tier 1 sources, or an official source (corporate site, government registry)
- `medium` — 1 Tier 1 source, or 2+ Tier 2 sources
- `low` — only 1 Tier 2 source, or indirect corroboration

If a fact only has `low` confidence, consider adding a note in the `notes` field of the output so the editor can verify.
