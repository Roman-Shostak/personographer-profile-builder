Profile Overview

  • Musk's wealth derives from majority and large minority stakes in his operating companies — principally SpaceX, Tesla, X Corp., and the artificial-intelligence company xAI — making him the wealthiest person in the world per Forbes (2025, 2026) and the Bloomberg Billionaires Index since 2025.
  • His business career began in 1995 with the co-founding of web-software company Zip2 with his brother Kimbal Musk, sold to Compaq in 1999 for $307 million.
  • In 1999 he co-founded the online financial-services company X.com, which merged with Confinity to form PayPal, acquired by eBay in 2002 for $1.5 billion; the same year he founded SpaceX, and in 2004 became chairman and majority shareholder of Tesla, taking the CEO role in 2008.
  • He went on to co-found OpenAI (2015), Neuralink (2016), The Boring Company (2017) and xAI (2023); in October 2022 he acquired Twitter for approximately $44 billion and rebranded the platform as X.
  • In November 2025 Tesla shareholders approved a performance-based compensation package potentially worth up to $1 trillion over a decade, and in February 2026 SpaceX acquired xAI in a $1.25 trillion combined-entity merger, ahead of an IPO targeted for mid-2026.
  • From January to May 2025 he served as a Special Government Employee leading the Department of Government Efficiency under the second Trump administration, departing after publicly opposing the administration's fiscal package.

Profile Q&A

What links Elon Musk's six operating companies into a single strategic posture?

Each ventures into a sector with high capital requirements and long payback horizons — aerospace, automotive electrification, AI compute, neurotechnology, tunneling and social media — and several are positioned to draw on each other's infrastructure, including the February 2026 SpaceX–xAI merger that placed AI compute inside the rocket maker.

How did Musk come to control Tesla, the company most associated with his name?

He invested $6.5 million in Tesla Motors in 2004, becoming chairman and the largest shareholder, and assumed the CEO role in October 2008 during the financial crisis; he has held it since.

What was the Department of Government Efficiency role in the second Trump administration?

From January 20 to late May 2025, Musk served as a Special Government Employee leading DOGE, the federal cost-cutting initiative that ran for approximately 130 days before his departure following public disagreement with the administration's spending bill.

How significant is the SpaceX–xAI merger announced in February 2026?

At a $1.25 trillion combined valuation, CNBC and Fortune described it as the largest corporate combination on record, and SpaceX has subsequently moved toward an IPO targeted for mid-2026 at a valuation of up to $1.5 trillion.

What is Musk's stated rationale for SpaceX's Mars program?

He has repeatedly framed the program in interviews and at industry events as an effort to make humanity multi-planetary, arguing that a self-sustaining off-world settlement functions as long-term insurance against existential risks to civilization on Earth.

Editorial Comment

Musk's institutional significance rests on the simultaneous operation, at scale, of companies across separately regulated industries — aerospace, automotive, artificial intelligence, neurotechnology, payments and social media — coordinated under his direct executive control. His career trajectory traces a recurring pattern: a dot-com cash-out (Zip2, PayPal) reinvested into capital-heavy industrial ventures (SpaceX, Tesla) that subsequently expanded into adjacent sectors (Starlink, energy storage, large language models). By 2026 that pattern has produced the first individual net worth above $800 billion, the largest corporate combination on record, the largest executive compensation plan ever approved, and a publicly held platform — X — that he uses to comment in real time on policy, markets and his own businesses. His five-month role leading the Department of Government Efficiency in the second Trump administration extended the same operating posture into federal administration, and ended in a public break with the President over the administration's fiscal package. The historical comparison most often drawn in serious coverage is to industrialists who consolidated multi-sector platforms in earlier eras of capital concentration; the open question reporters and analysts return to is whether the SpaceX–xAI structure can deliver on its stated long-horizon goals — orbital data centres, reusable Mars-capable launch systems and human-level artificial general intelligence — at the timelines he publicly commits to.
